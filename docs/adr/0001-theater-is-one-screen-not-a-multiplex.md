# A Theater represents one specific auditorium, not a whole multiplex

Real movie theaters are often multiplexes with many differently-specced screens (standard, IMAX, Dolby Cinema, etc.) under one location. We considered modeling a Theater as the whole complex, with a second-level picker for which screen inside it to view. We chose instead to make each catalog entry a single, specific, already-picked auditorium (e.g. "AMC Century City 15 - Auditorium 15", not "AMC Century City 15"). This maps directly onto the existing `Auditorium` type with no new nesting, and avoids building a two-level navigation UI to support screen-by-screen data we mostly can't verify anyway. The trade-off: a real complex with multiple notable screens needs one catalog entry per screen, with duplicated theater identity (name, city) across entries, rather than sharing it under one venue record.
