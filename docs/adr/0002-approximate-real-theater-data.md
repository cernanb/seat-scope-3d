# Real Theater data is a researched approximation, not exact fidelity

Theaters rarely publish exact screen dimensions, and real seat-for-seat maps are box-office UI data that changes with reseating and isn't something we can reliably source or keep in sync. We chose to approximate: research real screen width/height and total seating capacity where publicly available, then hand-author a plausible `rows` layout scaled to roughly match that capacity (not a formula applied uniformly, and not a transcription of the actual seat chart). We also chose to render every screen as flat, even for theaters with a real curved screen (e.g. TCL Chinese Theatre's IMAX screen), since curved geometry would require new 3D geometry and camera-framing work that's out of scope here. The app surfaces this honestly with a visible disclaimer and source citation per Theater, rather than presenting researched estimates as verified fact. If exact per-seat data or curved-screen rendering becomes valuable later, it's an additive change per Theater, not a rearchitecture.
